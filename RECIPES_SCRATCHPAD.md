## Design Constraints

- **Fire:** Most cooking is over open coals or a grill grate. A single medium pot is available.
- **Cold chain:** Proteins (shrimp, sausage) must be kept cold. No refrigeration on-site — cooler only.
- **No running water, no countertop, minimal utensils.** Pack-in / pack-out mentality.
- **Timing:** Recipes slot into the evening timeline (dinner, late night, pre-dawn).
- **Cross-contamination:** Dedicated skewer sets for pescatarian guests.
- **Dietary coverage needed:** gf, pescatarian, vegetarian — ideally vegan-adjacent where possible.
- **Ingredient economy:** Prefer ingredients that pull double or triple duty across multiple recipes.

### GRANDMA

Sweetheart, you've done a lovely job — proteins, tortillas pulling double duty, late-night snack, pre-dawn eggs. That's real planning. But there are a few holes.

**1. Campfire Foil Potatoes** *(the thing you're truly missing)*
You have no starch besides corn tortillas. After dark when it gets cold by the water, people want something heavy and warm. Slice 4-5 Yukon Golds thin, layer on double foil with onion rings, dot with butter, seal with a splash of water to steam. Medium coals 35-40 min, flip once. Open carefully — steam burns faster than fire. Throw cheese on top.
- **New ingredients:** Potatoes (5 medium Yukon Gold), heavy-duty aluminum foil
- **Tags:** vegetarian, gf — vegan without butter/cheese
- **Source:** Taste of Home Campfire Potatoes

**2. Banana Boats** *(a second dessert using what you already have)*
Slit a banana along the inner curve, stuff with chocolate pieces and mini marshmallows (both from your s'mores kit), wrap in foil, 10 min on coals. The banana goes soft, chocolate melts, marshmallows get gooey. Crush graham cracker on top. Bonus: bananas also serve as morning fruit.
- **New ingredients:** Bananas (4-6)
- **Tags:** vegetarian, gf
- **Source:** Fresh Off the Grid Campfire Banana Boats

**3. Mountain Man Breakfast Skillet** *(upgrade the pre-dawn slot)*
Plain eggs and cheese will have everyone hungry again by 9 AM. Brown cubed potatoes in butter in the pot, add peppers and onions, add chopped sausage, push to sides, scramble eggs with cheese in the middle, fold together. Hot sauce on top. Uses almost everything already on the list.
- **New ingredients:** None beyond potatoes from #1. Small bottle of cooking oil.
- **Tags:** gf — vegetarian without sausage
- **Source:** Fresh Off the Grid Mountain Man Breakfast Skillet

**4. Campfire Corn on the Cob** *(seasonal — buy en route)*
July–September, BC farm stands between Vancouver and the park will have fresh corn. Pull back husk, strip silk, fold husk back, soak 30 min in pot of water. On grate over medium coals, turn every 5 min, 25-30 min total. Butter and salt. Requires zero utensils to eat.
- **New ingredients:** Corn on the cob (8 ears, buy en route)
- **Tags:** vegetarian, gf — vegan without butter
- **Source:** Camping For Foodies — campfire sweet corn

**5. Black Bean & Corn Salsa** *(closes the vegan protein gap)*
Biggest gap in the book — no complete vegan dish. One can of black beans: drain, rinse, mix with corn, diced peppers, onion, lime + hot sauce (all already on the list). No cooking. Works as a topping on every savoury recipe. Maximum ingredient economy for one can.
- **New ingredients:** 1 can black beans, 1 can corn
- **Tags:** vegan, gf — every dietary box checked
- **Source:** Joyous Apron / Honey & Birch no-cook corn salsa

**Summary of new ingredients:** Potatoes, Bananas, Black beans (can), Corn (can or fresh en route), Cooking oil, Heavy-duty aluminum foil

#### 8. Campfire Oatmeal (Morning Breakfast)
- **Timing:** Morning (after 12h+) -- the REAL breakfast, not the pre-dawn one
- **Tags:** vegan, gf (use certified gf oats), nut-free
- **Effort:** Very Low
- **Source:** Frugal Campasaurus campfire oatmeal recipe (frugalcampasaurus.com)

**What it is:** Old-fashioned oats cooked in the one pot over the fire. Ratio is 1:2 oats to water. Bring water to a boil, stir in oats, cook 5 minutes stirring frequently, done. Top with brown sugar and cinnamon. Optional: dried fruit, a drizzle of the butter we already have.

**New ingredients needed:**
- Old-fashioned rolled oats (one canister, about 3 cups for the group) -- look for certified GF if needed
- Brown sugar (small bag, also useful for other things)
- Cinnamon (small shaker)
- Optional: raisins or dried cranberries

**Why it fills a gap:** Right now the only breakfast is the pre-dawn scrambled eggs, which is great at 5am but not a proper morning meal. Oatmeal is warm, filling, vegan by default, naturally gluten-free with the right oats, and uses only the pot we already have. Zero prep at home. Kids and adults both eat it. You can make it while coffee water heats. Brown sugar and cinnamon pack tiny and keep forever.

**Prep at home:** Pre-measure oats + brown sugar + cinnamon into a zip-lock bag per serving. At site, just add boiling water and stir.

#### 9. Black Bean & Veggie Tacos (Dinner, Vegan Main)
- **Timing:** Dinner -- serve alongside or instead of shrimp/sausage tacos
- **Tags:** vegan, gf, nut-free
- **Effort:** Low
- **Source:** Dahlia Kitchen corn & black bean tacos (dahliakitchen.com); Elavegan vegan quesadillas (elavegan.com)

**What it is:** Heat a can of drained black beans in the pot with our existing peppers and onions, a shake of chili powder and cumin, and a squeeze of lime. Spoon onto charred corn tortillas. Top with hot sauce and lime (already on the list).

**New ingredients needed:**
- 2 cans black beans (shelf-stable, no cooler space needed)
- Cumin (small amount -- could pre-measure into a tiny bag)
- Chili powder (small amount)
- Optional: 1 avocado (keeps fine in cooler for one night)

**Why it fills a gap:** This is the big one. Right now, a vegan guest at this trip has... peppers and onions? Maybe a plain tortilla? That is not a meal. Black beans in a corn tortilla with peppers, onions, lime, and hot sauce is a legitimate, filling, protein-rich dinner that reuses four ingredients we already carry (corn tortillas, peppers/onions, hot sauce, lime). The beans are shelf-stable so they don't compete for cooler space. The cumin and chili powder weigh nothing. This also works as a late-night option to bulk up the quesadillas -- fold beans and cheese into a tortilla and you have something much more substantial.

**Prep at home:** Chop an extra pepper and half an onion, bag them with the dinner veg. Pre-measure spices into a tiny zip-lock. That is it.

#### 10. Campfire Hash Brown Packets (Morning Breakfast, Substantial)
- **Timing:** Morning (after 12h+) -- serve alongside oatmeal or eggs
- **Tags:** vegetarian, gf, nut-free (as written; add sausage for omnivore version)
- **Effort:** Low
- **Source:** Taste of Home campfire cheese hash brown packets (tasteofhome.com)

**What it is:** Frozen shredded hash brown potatoes (the O'Brien style with peppers already mixed in, or plain), tossed with shredded cheese, salt, and pepper, divided into foil packets. Cook on the grill grate over coals, 6-9 minutes per side, until potatoes are tender and cheese is melted. Open carefully (steam!), eat straight from the foil.

**New ingredients needed:**
- 1 bag (28 oz) frozen hash brown potatoes -- these go in the cooler but they also HELP keep the cooler cold, so they are doing double duty
- Heavy-duty aluminum foil (you need this anyway for banana boats, see below)

**Why it fills a gap:** Morning substance. The oatmeal covers the vegan/light eaters, but some people (teenagers, anyone who stayed up late tending the fire) need carbs and cheese and something savory. Hash browns in foil are zero-skill, no-dishes, and they use the cheese we already have. They also work as a side at dinner if you want to round out a meal. The frozen potatoes act as ice packs in the cooler during transport -- genuine double duty.

**Prep at home:** None needed, just buy the bag frozen. If you want to be fancy, pre-portion into foil packets at home, toss with cheese and seasoning, stack them in the cooler, and cook straight from cold at the site.

#### 11. Make-Ahead Breakfast Burritos (Morning Breakfast, Grab-and-Go)
- **Timing:** Morning (or honestly any time someone is hungry and the fire is going)
- **Tags:** vegetarian base, gf if using corn tortillas (note: corn tortillas crack when rolled -- see tip below)
- **Effort:** Very Low at site (all effort is at home)
- **Source:** Campfire Foodie camping breakfast burritos (campfirefoodie.com)

**What it is:** Scrambled eggs, cheese, sauteed peppers and onions, rolled in tortillas, wrapped individually in foil. Made entirely at home the night before. At the site, place foil-wrapped burritos near the coals (not IN the coals -- beside them, or on the grill grate over low heat) and turn once. 10-15 minutes until heated through.

**New ingredients needed:**
- None for the filling -- uses eggs, cheese, peppers, onions, and butter/garlic we already carry
- Flour tortillas for rolling (corn tortillas crack and split when you try to roll a burrito -- they work for folded tacos and quesadillas, but for a stuffed burrito you need flour; buy a small pack of 8)
- Note: flour tortillas are NOT gluten-free, so label these clearly for the GF folks

**Why it fills a gap:** This is the "insurance policy" recipe. If it is raining, if nobody wants to cook, if someone wakes up starving at 7am before the fire is really going -- you just pull a foil packet out of the cooler and warm it up. It uses ingredients we are already buying. The prep happens in your own kitchen with running water and a real stove. At the site, the effort is literally "put foil near heat, wait, eat." Make 6-8 of these and you have covered breakfast for everyone with almost no on-site work.

**Prep at home:** Scramble eggs with butter, garlic, peppers, onions; fold in cheese off heat. Cool. Roll into flour tortillas, wrap each one tightly in foil. Store in cooler. They keep fine for 24 hours in a cold cooler.

**GF workaround:** For GF folks, just make the scrambled eggs fresh at the site (recipe 6 already covers this) and serve the filling in a corn tortilla as an open-face taco instead of a rolled burrito.

#### 12. Banana Boats (Dessert, Fruit-Based)
- **Timing:** After dinner or late night -- whenever the coals are right
- **Tags:** vegetarian, gf, nut-free (as written with our existing s'mores ingredients)
- **Effort:** Very Low
- **Source:** Fresh Off The Grid campfire banana boats (freshoffthegrid.com)

**What it is:** Take a banana with the peel still on. Slice it lengthwise along the inside curve -- cut deep but not through the bottom peel. Pull it open slightly. Stuff the slit with chocolate chips (or broken chocolate bar pieces) and mini marshmallows. Wrap in foil. Set on coals for 10 minutes. Open foil, crush a graham cracker on top. Eat with a spoon straight from the peel.

**New ingredients needed:**
- Bananas (buy 4-6, slightly green so they don't bruise in transport)
- That is literally it -- chocolate, marshmallows, and graham crackers are already on the list for s'mores

**Why it fills a gap:** This reuses THREE existing dessert ingredients and adds one cheap, easy-to-pack item (bananas). It gives you a second dessert option that has actual fruit in it, which makes me feel slightly better about the sugar situation. It is also more fun and novel than s'mores for kids who have done s'mores a hundred times. The warm banana gets caramelized and gooey and it is genuinely delicious. Plus: bananas that do not get used for dessert become breakfast (slice onto oatmeal, eat plain with coffee). Zero waste.

**Prep at home:** Buy bananas. Done.
